Chromium - Element Details

Symbol: Cr

Atomic Number: 24

Atomic Mass: 51.9961 u

Electron Configuration: [Ar] 3d⁵ 4s¹

Group: 6 (Transition Metals)

Period: 4

Block: d-block

Category: Transition Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Silvery, metallic, and lustrous

Density: ~7.19 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~1860 °C

Boiling Point: ~2671 °C

Discovery

Chromium was discovered by French chemist Louis Nicolas Vauquelin in 1797. He isolated the element from a mineral known as Siberian red lead (crocoite).

Abundance & Sources

Chromium is the 21st most abundant element in the Earth's crust. It is primarily found in the mineral chromite (FeCr₂O₄), which is mined mainly in South Africa, Kazakhstan, India, and Turkey.

Industrial Preparation

Chromium metal is produced from chromite ore. The ore is first heated with a mixture of lime and sodium carbonate in a rotary kiln, forming sodium chromate. This is then converted to chromium oxide (Cr₂O₃), which is reduced with aluminum or silicon at high temperatures to produce pure chromium.

Uses

  • Major component in stainless steel, providing its corrosion resistance and lustrous finish (e.g., in cutlery, kitchenware, and industrial equipment).
  • Used for chrome plating, which provides a hard, corrosion-resistant, and shiny surface to other metals.
  • Chromium compounds are used as pigments in paints (e.g., chrome yellow), ceramics, and glass.
  • Used in the tanning of leather.
  • Some chromium compounds are essential micronutrients (e.g., in glucose metabolism), though hexavalent chromium is toxic.

Interesting Facts

  • The name "chromium" comes from the Greek word "chroma", meaning "color", due to the wide variety of colors its compounds can exhibit.
  • It is the third hardest element after carbon (diamond) and boron.
  • Chromium plating is a very thin layer (often less than a micrometer thick).

Did You Know?

The brilliant red color of rubies comes from trace amounts of chromium impurities within the corundum crystal lattice.
