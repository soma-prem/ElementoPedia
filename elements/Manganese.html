Manganese - Element Details

Symbol: Mn

Atomic Number: 25

Atomic Mass: 54.938 u

Electron Configuration: [Ar] 3d⁵ 4s²

Group: 7 (Transition Metals)

Period: 4

Category: Transition Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Density: ~7.21 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~1246 °C

Boiling Point: ~2061 °C

Discovery

Manganese was discovered by Swedish chemist Carl Wilhelm Scheele in 1774, and first isolated by Johan Gottlieb Gahn later that same year.

Abundance & Sources

Manganese is the 12th most abundant element in the Earth's crust. It is primarily found in minerals such as pyrolusite (MnO₂) and rhodochrosite (MnCO₃).

Uses

  • Major component in steel production (ferromanganese and silicomanganese), improving strength, hardness, and wear resistance.
  • Used in aluminum alloys to improve corrosion resistance.
  • Manganese dioxide is used in dry cell batteries (alkaline and zinc-carbon batteries).
  • Manganese compounds are used as pigments, oxidizers, and in agriculture as a fertilizer component.

Interesting Facts

  • Manganese is essential for human health, playing a role in bone formation, metabolism, and antioxidant defense.
  • It gives amethyst its characteristic purple color.
  • Despite its importance in industry, manganese metal is quite brittle.

Did You Know?

The largest reserves of manganese are found on the ocean floor in manganese nodules, which are potato-sized concretions containing various metals.
