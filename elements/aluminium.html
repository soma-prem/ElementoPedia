Aluminium – Element Details

Symbol: Al

Atomic Number: 13

Atomic Mass: 26.982 u

Electron Configuration: [Ne] 3s2 3p1

Group: 13 (Boron group)

Period: 3

Block: p‑block

Category: Post‑transition metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Silvery‑white

Melting Point: 660.32 °C

Boiling Point: ~2519 °C

Discovery

Discovered in the 1750s by Andreas Marggraf, first isolated as metal by Hans Christian Ørsted in 1825. Mass production began in 1886 via the Hall‑Héroult process. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Abundance & Sources

It’s the most abundant metallic element in Earth's crust (~8%) and is extracted from bauxite ore. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Uses

  • Structural material in aerospace, automotive, architecture
  • Packaging (foil, cans), wiring, cookware
  • Alloys (e.g. duralumin) for strength and lightweight

Interesting Facts

  • It self‑passivates with an oxide layer, preventing corrosion. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  • It has low density—about one‑third that of steel. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Did You Know?

Aluminium plays vital roles in aviation and packaging, and is fully recyclable with minimal degradation.
