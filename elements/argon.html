Argon – Element Details

Symbol: Ar

Atomic Number: 18

Atomic Mass: 39.948 u

Electron Configuration: [Ne] 3s2 3p6

Group: 18 (Noble gases)

Period: 3

Category: Noble gas

State at Room Temperature: Gas

Color: Colorless

Melting Point: −189.3 °C

Boiling Point: −185.8 °C

Density: ≈ 1.784 g/L at 0 °C

Discovery

Discovered in 1894 by Lord Rayleigh and Sir William Ramsay while studying nitrogen from air. It was the first noble gas discovered.

Abundance & Sources

Argon makes up about 0.93% of the Earth’s atmosphere. It is obtained by the fractional distillation of liquid air.

Uses

  • Used as an inert shielding gas in welding and other high-temperature processes
  • Fills incandescent and fluorescent light bulbs
  • Used in double-pane windows as an insulating layer

Interesting Facts

  • Argon is chemically inert and does not form compounds easily
  • It gets its name from the Greek word "argos" meaning lazy or inactive
  • Despite being inert, it glows violet in electric discharge tubes

Did You Know?

Argon was originally considered to be a contaminant in nitrogen experiments until its identity as a new element was confirmed.
