Barium - Element Details

Symbol: Ba

Atomic Number: 56

Atomic Mass: 137.327 u

Electron Configuration: [Xe] 6s²

Group: 2 (Alkaline Earth Metals)

Period: 6

Block: s-block

Category: Alkaline Earth Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Silvery-white, metallic luster

Density: ~3.59 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~727 °C

Boiling Point: ~1897 °C

Discovery

Barium was first identified as a new element in 1774 by Swedish chemist Carl Wilhelm Scheele in a new mineral he discovered. However, it was not isolated as a pure metal until 1808, when English chemist Sir Humphry Davy produced it through the electrolysis of molten barium compounds. Davy named the element "barium" from the Greek word "barys," meaning "heavy," because of the high density of its mineral, barite.

Abundance & Sources

Barium is a moderately abundant element, making up about 0.05% of the Earth's crust. It is never found in its free form due to its high reactivity. The main commercial source is the mineral barite (barium sulfate, $BaSO_4$), with the major deposits in China, India, and the United States.

Industrial Preparation

Barium metal is produced by the reduction of barium oxide with aluminum or silicon in a vacuum at high temperatures. Barium sulfate from barite is first converted to barium sulfide by heating it with carbon, and then the sulfide is converted to the oxide.

Uses

  • Barium sulfate is used as a radiocontrast agent for medical imaging of the gastrointestinal tract, known as a "barium meal."
  • It is a key component in the drilling fluids used in oil and gas wells, helping to increase the density of the fluid to prevent blowouts.
  • Barium nitrate and barium chlorate are used to produce the green color in fireworks.
  • Barium is used as a getter in vacuum tubes and as a component in alloys for spark plugs.
  • Barium titanate is an important component in some capacitors and piezoelectric ceramics.

Interesting Facts

  • Barium is a very reactive metal and is always found in nature in its combined state.
  • While barium sulfate is safe for use in medical imaging because of its low solubility, most other barium compounds are highly toxic and can cause heart problems and paralysis.
  • The mineral barite is famously known as a "heavy spar" because of its high density, which is about four times that of water.

Did You Know?

The use of barium sulfate in medical imaging is a classic example of a "chemical paradox." A compound containing a highly toxic element is used safely because the specific compound is insoluble and therefore not absorbed by the body.
