Boron - Element Details

Symbol: B

Atomic Number: 5

Atomic Mass: 10.81 u

Electron Configuration: 1s2 2s2 2p1

Group: 13 (Metalloids)

Period: 2

Block: p-block

Category: Metalloid

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Black-brown

Melting Point: 2076 °C

Boiling Point: 3927 °C

Discovery

Boron was discovered independently by Sir Humphry Davy, Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, and Louis Jacques Thénard in 1808 through the electrolysis of borates.

Abundance & Sources

Boron is not found free in nature but occurs in compounds such as borax, kernite, and colemanite. Major sources include Turkey and the United States.

Industrial Preparation

Pure boron is prepared by reducing boron halides with hydrogen or by metallothermic reduction of borates using magnesium or aluminum.

Uses

  • Used in glass and ceramics (borosilicate glass).
  • Essential in fertilizers for plant growth.
  • Used in detergents and cleaning agents.
  • Used in neutron absorbers in nuclear reactors.

Interesting Facts

  • Boron is a metalloid – it has properties of both metals and non-metals.
  • It's vital in small amounts for plant and possibly human health.
  • Boron fibers are strong and used in advanced aerospace materials.

Did You Know?

Boron nitride is nearly as hard as diamond and used in abrasives and cutting tools. Boron also helps regulate hormone levels in the human body!
