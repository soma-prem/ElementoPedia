Calcium – Element Details

Symbol: Ca

Atomic Number: 20

Atomic Mass: 40.078 u

Electron Configuration: [Ar] 4s2

Group: 2 (Alkaline Earth Metals)

Period: 4

Category: Alkaline Earth Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Silvery-gray

Melting Point: 842 °C

Boiling Point: 1484 °C

Density: ~1.54 g/cm³

Discovery

Pure calcium was isolated by Sir Humphry Davy in 1808 via electrolysis of its oxide, though calcium compounds (lime) were used since ancient times :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.

Abundance & Sources

Calcium is the fifth most abundant element in Earth’s crust (~3%) and third most abundant metal. It is mainly found in limestone (CaCO₃), gypsum, fluorite, and apatite :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}.

Uses

  • Used in steelmaking as deoxidizer and desulfurizer.
  • Calcium compounds are used in cement, plaster, and building materials.
  • Used in dietary supplements and antacids.

Interesting Facts

  • Essential for bone and tooth health in living organisms.
  • Bright yellow‑red flame test indicates calcium presence.
  • Name derives from Latin “calx” meaning lime :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}.

Did You Know?

Calcium is used in automotive batteries as alloying element to reduce maintenance and improve safety.
