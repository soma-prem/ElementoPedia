Californium - Element Details

Symbol: Cf

Atomic Number: 98

Atomic Mass: [251] u

Electron Configuration: [Rn] 5f¹⁰ 7s²

Group: 3 (Actinides)

Period: 7

Block: f-block

Category: Actinide

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Silvery-white metal

Density: ~15.1 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~900 °C

Boiling Point: ~1470 °C

Discovery

Californium was discovered in 1950 by a team of scientists led by **Glenn T. Seaborg** at the University of California, Berkeley. They synthesized it by bombarding a tiny sample of curium-242 with alpha particles (helium nuclei). The element was named after the state of **California** and the university where the discovery was made.

Abundance & Sources

Californium is a **synthetic, radioactive element** that does not exist in nature. It is produced in extremely small, microscopic quantities in nuclear reactors and laboratories. Only a few grams are produced worldwide each year, making it one of the most expensive and rare elements.

Industrial Preparation

The industrial preparation of californium is a highly specialized process. It is produced by bombarding curium with neutrons in a high-flux isotope reactor. The process involves a series of nuclear reactions and beta decays, eventually yielding minute quantities of californium isotopes.

Uses

  • The primary use of californium is as a powerful **neutron source**. The isotope **Californium-252** is a strong neutron emitter through spontaneous fission.
  • This neutron source is used in a variety of applications, including **neutron activation analysis** to detect elements in materials, moisture gauges for finding water in soil, and in portable metal detectors.
  • In medicine, californium-252 is used for treating certain types of **tumors** in radiotherapy.
  • It is also used to start nuclear reactors.

Interesting Facts

  • Californium-252 is one of the most powerful neutron emitters known. A single microgram of the isotope can produce millions of neutrons per second.
  • It is so rare and expensive that it is typically sold by the microgram.
  • Due to its intense radioactivity, handling californium requires extensive safety precautions.

Did You Know?

A tiny amount of Californium-252, about the size of a pinhead, can emit millions of neutrons per second. This makes it an ideal portable neutron source for a variety of industrial and medical applications where a compact and powerful source of neutrons is needed.
