Carbon - Element Details

Symbol: C

Atomic Number: 6

Atomic Mass: 12.011 u

Electron Configuration: 1s2 2s2 2p2

Group: 14 (Carbon Group)

Period: 2

Block: p-block

Category: Non-metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Black (graphite), transparent (diamond)

Melting Point: ~3550 °C (sublimes)

Boiling Point: ~4027 °C

Discovery

Carbon has been known since ancient times in the form of charcoal and soot. It was recognized as an element in the 18th century.

Abundance & Sources

Carbon is the fourth most abundant element in the universe by mass and is found in all known life forms. It's present in the atmosphere as CO₂, in fossil fuels, and in carbonates like limestone.

Allotropes

  • Diamond: Hardest known natural material, transparent.
  • Graphite: Soft, black, and conducts electricity.
  • Fullerenes: Spherical molecules like C60.
  • Graphene: One-atom-thick sheet, very strong and conductive.

Uses

  • As a fuel (coal, petroleum).
  • In steel production.
  • As graphite in pencils and lubricants.
  • As diamonds in cutting tools and jewelry.
  • In electronics and nanotechnology (graphene, carbon nanotubes).

Interesting Facts

  • Carbon forms more compounds than any other element.
  • It's essential to life — present in proteins, fats, DNA, and sugars.
  • Carbon dating is used to determine the age of archaeological samples.

Did You Know?

Graphene, a form of carbon just one atom thick, is 200 times stronger than steel and conducts electricity better than copper!
