Chlorine – Element Details

Symbol: Cl

Atomic Number: 17

Atomic Mass: 35.45 u

Electron Configuration: [Ne] 3s2 3p5

Group: 17 (Halogens)

Period: 3

Category: Non-metal

State at Room Temperature: Gas

Color: Pale yellow-green

Melting Point: -101 °C

Boiling Point: -34 °C

Density: 3.2 g/L (gas)

Discovery

Discovered by Carl Wilhelm Scheele in 1774. Officially recognized as an element by Humphry Davy in 1810.

Uses

  • Water purification and disinfection
  • Bleach production
  • Plastics (PVC)

Interesting Facts

  • Essential for producing hydrochloric acid (HCl)
  • Used in making medicines and pesticides

Did You Know?

Chlorine is poisonous at high concentrations and was used as a chemical weapon in World War I.
