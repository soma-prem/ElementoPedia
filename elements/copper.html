Copper - Element Details

Symbol: Cu

Atomic Number: 29

Atomic Mass: 63.546 u

Electron Configuration: [Ar] 3d¹⁰ 4s¹

Group: 11 (Transition Metals)

Period: 4

Block: d-block

Category: Transition Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Reddish-orange metallic luster

Density: ~8.96 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~1085 °C

Boiling Point: ~2562 °C

Discovery

Copper has been known and used by humans for at least 10,000 years, making it one of the oldest metals discovered. Its use dates back to the Neolithic period, and it was the first metal to be smelted from its ores.

Abundance & Sources

Copper is the 25th most abundant element in the Earth's crust. It is found in various minerals, including chalcopyrite, bornite, and cuprite. Chile, Peru, and China are among the largest copper producers.

Industrial Preparation

Copper is primarily extracted from sulfide ores through a multi-step process involving mining, crushing, grinding, froth flotation (to concentrate the ore), smelting (to produce blister copper), and finally refining, often through electrolysis to achieve high purity.

Uses

  • Widely used in electrical wiring due to its excellent electrical conductivity.
  • Used extensively in plumbing and roofing due to its corrosion resistance and malleability.
  • A key component in various alloys such as brass (copper and zinc), bronze (copper and tin), and cupronickel (copper and nickel).
  • Used in coinage, decorative arts, and statuary.
  • Essential micronutrient for living organisms.

Interesting Facts

  • Copper is an excellent conductor of heat and electricity, second only to silver among metals.
  • It is naturally antimicrobial, meaning it can kill bacteria and viruses on its surface.
  • The Statue of Liberty is covered with a thin layer of copper, which has oxidized over time to form its iconic green patina.

Did You Know?

The "Bronze Age" was a period characterized by the widespread use of bronze, an alloy of copper (typically with tin), for tools and weapons, demonstrating the early importance of copper in human civilization.
