Erbium - Element Details

Symbol: Er

Atomic Number: 68

Atomic Mass: 167.259 u

Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f¹² 6s²

Group: 3 (Lanthanides)

Period: 6

Block: f-block

Category: Lanthanide

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Silvery-white, metallic luster

Density: ~9.066 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~1529 °C

Boiling Point: ~2868 °C

Discovery

Erbium was discovered in 1843 by Swedish chemist **Carl Gustaf Mosander**. While analyzing a sample of the mineral gadolinite, he was able to separate the yttrium oxide component into three new elements, which he named terbia, erbia, and yttria. He named the element Erbium after the village of **Ytterby**, Sweden, the same location that also gave its name to yttrium, terbium, and ytterbium.

Abundance & Sources

Erbium is a rare-earth element, never found in its pure form in nature. It is typically found in rare-earth minerals such as xenotime and monazite, alongside other lanthanides. The primary sources for these minerals are located in China, the United States, and Australia.

Industrial Preparation

The industrial production of erbium involves a series of complex separation steps to isolate it from the other rare-earth elements in the ore. The separation is primarily achieved using solvent extraction or ion-exchange chromatography. The pure metal is then produced by the reduction of anhydrous erbium fluoride ($ErF_3$) with calcium metal at high temperatures.

Uses

  • Erbium is a key component in **fiber optic communications**, particularly in Erbium-doped fiber amplifiers (EDFAs) that amplify light signals without needing to convert them to electricity.
  • In the medical field, **erbium-doped lasers (Er:YAG lasers)** are used for various applications, including skin resurfacing, dental surgery, and plastic surgery.
  • Erbium is used as a colorant in glass and porcelain enamel, where it imparts a distinct **pink color**.
  • Its compounds are also used in phosphors for displays and in some metallurgical applications to improve the ductility of metals.

Interesting Facts

  • Erbium is a soft, malleable, silvery-white metal that is relatively stable in air compared to other rare-earth metals.
  • The pink color produced by erbium ions in glass and crystals is a defining characteristic of the element.
  • Erbium-doped optical fibers are so efficient at amplifying light that they form the backbone of modern long-haul fiber optic networks, allowing data to be transmitted across oceans and continents.

Did You Know?

The village of Ytterby, Sweden, is home to a mineral mine that has provided the source material for the discovery of four different elements: Yttrium, Terbium, Erbium, and Ytterbium. This makes it the most prolific single location for element discovery in the world.
