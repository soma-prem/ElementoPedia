Gold - Element Details

Symbol: Au

Atomic Number: 79

Atomic Mass: 196.96657 u

Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f¹⁴ 5d¹⁰ 6s¹

Group: 11 (Transition Metals)

Period: 6

Block: d-block

Category: Transition Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Metallic yellow

Density: ~19.32 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~1064 °C

Boiling Point: ~2856 °C

Discovery

Gold has been known and highly valued since prehistoric times. It is one of the **"seven metals of antiquity"** and its discovery is not attributed to a single person. It has been used for coinage, jewelry, and art for thousands of years by civilizations across the globe, including ancient Egypt, Mesopotamia, and the Roman Empire.

Abundance & Sources

Gold is a rare element in the Earth's crust. It is often found in its native, metallic state, either as nuggets or microscopic particles embedded in rock formations or alluvial deposits. The largest gold-producing countries are China, Australia, and Russia. Gold can also be found in trace amounts in seawater.

Industrial Preparation

The extraction of gold involves several methods. Historically, panning was used to find gold in alluvial deposits. Modern industrial methods include cyanide processing, where a dilute cyanide solution is used to leach gold from the ore. The gold is then recovered from the solution using zinc dust or carbon adsorption. A process called amalgamation, using mercury, was also historically common but is now largely avoided due to its environmental toxicity.

Uses

  • The primary use of gold is in **jewelry**, accounting for over 50% of the global demand.
  • It is also used as a **store of value** and an investment in the form of coins and bullion.
  • Due to its excellent electrical conductivity and corrosion resistance, gold is used in **electronics** for connectors, contacts, and wiring in high-end devices like computers and smartphones.
  • In **dentistry**, gold is used for fillings, crowns, and bridges due to its inertness and malleability.
  • It is used in a variety of other applications, including in medical treatments, glass production, and aerospace engineering.

Interesting Facts

  • Gold is the most malleable and ductile of all metals. A single gram of gold can be hammered into a sheet one square meter in size, or drawn into a wire over a mile long.
  • It is a noble metal, meaning it is highly resistant to corrosion, oxidation, and most acids. Only a mixture of nitric and hydrochloric acids (aqua regia) can dissolve it.
  • The purity of gold is measured in **karats**. 24-karat gold is 99.9% pure, while 18-karat gold is 75% gold and 25% other metals.

Did You Know?

Due to its inertness and resistance to corrosion, gold is used in surgical implants and other medical devices that need to be in contact with the human body for extended periods without causing an immune response.
