Iridium - Element Details

Symbol: Ir

Atomic Number: 77

Atomic Mass: 192.217 u

Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f¹⁴ 5d⁷ 6s²

Group: 9 (Transition Metals)

Period: 6

Block: d-block

Category: Transition Metal, Platinum Group Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Silvery-white, lustrous metal

Density: ~22.56 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~2446 °C

Boiling Point: ~4428 °C

Discovery

Iridium was discovered in 1803 by English chemist **Smithson Tennant**. While working with a residue left over after dissolving platinum ore in aqua regia, he noticed a new, insoluble metallic element. He named it Iridium from the Latin word *iris*, meaning "**rainbow**," because of the wide variety of colors its salts exhibited in solution.

Abundance & Sources

Iridium is one of the rarest elements in the Earth's crust. It is a member of the platinum group metals and is primarily found in alloys with osmium. It is obtained commercially as a byproduct of nickel and copper refining from ores found in South Africa, Russia, and Canada.

Industrial Preparation

The extraction and purification of iridium is a complex and difficult process due to its chemical inertness and very low abundance. It is separated from other platinum group metals through a series of chemical processes, often involving heating with salts and acids to form soluble compounds, which are then precipitated and reduced to the pure metal.

Uses

  • Iridium is used in **high-temperature crucibles**, especially for growing synthetic crystals used in electronics.
  • Due to its extreme durability, it is used in **spark plug tips** for aircraft engines and in certain high-performance electrical contacts.
  • An **iridium-platinum alloy** was historically used to create the standard international meter bar and kilogram mass, due to its resistance to corrosion and wear.
  • Iridium is a catalyst in certain chemical reactions and is used in a variety of specialized applications, including in deep-space telescopes.

Interesting Facts

  • Iridium is the **most corrosion-resistant metal** known to man. It is unaffected by most acids and alkalis.
  • It is the **second-densest element**, with a density of 22.56 g/cm³, just slightly less than osmium.
  • Iridium is a relatively common element in asteroids, and the high concentration of iridium in the geological layer known as the **K-T boundary** provides strong evidence for a major asteroid impact that led to the extinction of the dinosaurs.

Did You Know?

The discovery of the iridium anomaly at the K-T boundary was one of the most significant scientific discoveries of the 20th century. This thin layer, found worldwide, contains a concentration of iridium far higher than what is typical for Earth's crust, pointing to an extraterrestrial origin.
