Iron - Element Details

Symbol: Fe

Atomic Number: 26

Atomic Mass: 55.845 u

Electron Configuration: [Ar] 3d⁶ 4s²

Group: 8 (Transition Metals)

Period: 4

Category: Transition Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Density: ~7.874 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~1538 °C

Boiling Point: ~2862 °C

Discovery

Iron has been known since ancient times. Its use by humans dates back to prehistoric times, with the earliest iron objects found dating from around 3200 BC in Egypt.

Abundance & Sources

Iron is the fourth most abundant element in the Earth's crust and the most abundant metal. It is primarily extracted from iron ores like hematite (Fe₂O₃) and magnetite (Fe₃O₄) through smelting in blast furnaces.

Uses

  • Primary component of steel, an essential material for construction, infrastructure, vehicles, and machinery.
  • Used in cast iron for pipes, automotive parts, and cookware.
  • Important in biological systems, particularly as a component of hemoglobin in blood, which carries oxygen.
  • Used in various industrial catalysts.

Interesting Facts

  • The Earth's core is believed to be composed primarily of iron and nickel.
  • Pure iron is relatively soft, but its strength is dramatically increased when alloyed with carbon to form steel.
  • Iron is ferromagnetic, meaning it can be magnetized, a property crucial for many electrical applications.

Did You Know?

The Iron Age, following the Bronze Age, marked a significant period in human history characterized by the widespread use of iron tools and weapons.
