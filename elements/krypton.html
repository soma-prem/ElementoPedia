Krypton - Element Details

Symbol: Kr

Atomic Number: 36

Atomic Mass: 83.798 u

Electron Configuration: [Ar] 3d¹⁰ 4s² 4p⁶

Group: 18 (Noble Gases)

Period: 4

Block: p-block

Category: Noble Gas

State at Room Temperature: Gas

Color: Colorless

Odor: Odorless

Density: ~3.749 g/L (at STP)

Melting Point: -157.36 °C

Boiling Point: -153.22 °C

Discovery

Krypton was discovered in 1898 by Scottish chemist Sir William Ramsay and English chemist Morris Travers. They isolated it by fractional distillation of liquid air, alongside neon and xenon, and named it from the Greek word "kryptos," meaning "the hidden one."

Abundance & Sources

Krypton is a very rare element in the Earth's atmosphere, constituting only about 1 part per million. It is a non-renewable resource found in trace amounts in the air.

Industrial Preparation

Krypton is obtained commercially by the fractional distillation of liquid air. Since it is present in such small quantities, this process is energy-intensive and makes krypton relatively expensive.

Uses

  • Used in certain types of electric lights, such as fluorescent lamps, high-speed photography flashes, and some incandescent lights for longer lifespan and higher efficiency.
  • Krypton-fluoride lasers are used in nuclear fusion research.
  • Used in some specialized gas discharge lamps for light shows and advertising.
  • Because of its lower thermal conductivity, it's used as a filler gas in some double-paned windows for better insulation.

Interesting Facts

  • Krypton is one of the noble gases, known for its extreme chemical inertness.
  • It emits a white light when an electric current passes through it, making it useful in certain lighting applications.
  • For a short period, the meter was defined by the wavelength of light emitted by Krypton-86, before being redefined in terms of the speed of light.

Did You Know?

Despite its rarity, krypton has played a role in science fiction, famously as "Kryptonite" in the Superman comics, although the real element is a harmless noble gas and not a radioactive mineral from outer space!
