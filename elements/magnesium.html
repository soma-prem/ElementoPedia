Magnesium - Element Details

Symbol: Mg

Atomic Number: 12

Atomic Mass: 24.305 u

Electron Configuration: [Ne] 3s2

Group: 2 (Alkaline Earth Metals)

Period: 3

Block: s-block

Category: Alkaline Earth Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Density: ~1.74 g/cm³

Melting Point: 650 °C (≈923 K)

Boiling Point: 1090 °C (≈1363 K)

Discovery

Magnesium oxide (“magnesia alba”) was recognized as a distinct substance by Joseph Black in 1755. Metallic magnesium was isolated in 1808 by Sir Humphry Davy through electrolysis :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.

Abundance & Sources

Magnesium is the eighth most abundant element in Earth's crust and makes up ~13% of Earth's mass. It is widely extracted from minerals such as magnesite and dolomite, and from seawater :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.

Uses

  • Alloyed with aluminum and zinc to create strong, lightweight materials used in aerospace and automotive industries :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.
  • Burns with brilliant white flame; used in flares, fireworks, and photographic flash bulbs :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.
  • Used medically as antacids (milk of magnesia) and laxatives, and to regulate nerve/muscle function :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}.
  • Used as a sacrificial anode to protect steel structures from corrosion :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}.

Interesting Facts

  • Magnesium ions are essential for over 300 enzyme reactions, including ATP processing :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}.
  • It is critical in the chlorophyll of plants for photosynthesis :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}.
  • Magnesium has one of the highest strength-to-weight ratios among structural metals :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}.

Did You Know?

Magnesium played a key role in World War I lighting (star shells), and its production economy grew rapidly due to wartime need :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}.
