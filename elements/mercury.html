Mercury - Element Details

Symbol: Hg

Atomic Number: 80

Atomic Mass: 200.592 u

Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f¹⁴ 5d¹⁰ 6s²

Group: 12 (Transition Metals)

Period: 6

Block: d-block

Category: Transition Metal

State at Room Temperature: Liquid

Color: Silvery-white, heavy liquid

Density: ~13.534 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~-38.83 °C

Boiling Point: ~356.73 °C

Discovery

Mercury has been known and used since antiquity. It has been found in ancient Egyptian tombs from as far back as 1500 BCE. Its properties were known to ancient Greeks, Romans, Chinese, and Indians. Its symbol, Hg, comes from its Latinized Greek name, *hydrargyrum*, meaning "**water-silver**," in reference to its liquid metallic nature.

Abundance & Sources

Mercury is a rare element in the Earth's crust. The primary ore from which it is extracted is **cinnabar** (mercuric sulfide, $HgS$). The main sources of cinnabar are in Spain, Italy, and China, with Spain's Almadén mine being the most famous historical source.

Industrial Preparation

The industrial production of mercury is a relatively simple process. The ore cinnabar is heated in air, which causes the mercuric sulfide to decompose. The mercury is vaporized and then collected and condensed in a cooler part of the furnace to be collected as a liquid. This process releases highly toxic sulfur dioxide gas, which must be carefully contained.

Uses

  • Historically, mercury was used extensively in **thermometers, barometers, and sphygmomanometers** (blood pressure monitors). This use is now heavily restricted due to its toxicity.
  • It is still used in some **dental amalgams** for fillings, though this use is also declining.
  • Mercury is a key component in **fluorescent lights** and some other specialized types of lighting.
  • It is used in some chemical catalysts, particularly in the production of certain plastics.
  • The element's high density and liquid state have made it useful in certain industrial switches and relays.

Interesting Facts

  • Mercury is the **only metallic element that is a liquid** at standard temperature and pressure. Only bromine is a liquid non-metal.
  • It is a heavy, silvery-white liquid. Due to its high surface tension, it forms round beads when spilled on a flat surface.
  • Mercury is highly **toxic**, and its vapor is particularly dangerous when inhaled. Prolonged exposure can cause serious neurological damage and kidney problems.

Did You Know?

The phrase "**mad as a hatter**" is believed to originate from mercury poisoning. Hat makers in the 18th and 19th centuries used mercury compounds to treat felt, and the prolonged exposure to mercury vapor caused neurological damage, including tremors, shyness, and irritability.
