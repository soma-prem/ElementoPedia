Neon - Element Details

Symbol: Ne

Atomic Number: 10

Atomic Mass: 20.1797 u

Electron Configuration: 1s2 2s2 2p6

Group: 18 (Noble Gases)

Period: 2

Block: p-block

Category: Noble Gas

State at Room Temperature: Gas

Color: Colorless (glows reddish-orange in discharge tubes)

Melting Point: -248.59 °C

Boiling Point: -246.08 °C

Discovery

Neon was discovered in 1898 by Sir William Ramsay and Morris W. Travers in London by studying liquefied air. They isolated neon by fractional distillation.

Abundance & Sources

Neon is rare on Earth but abundant in the universe. It is obtained commercially by liquefying air and performing fractional distillation.

Uses

  • Used in neon signs and lighting due to its bright glow when electrified.
  • Used in high-voltage indicators and vacuum tubes.
  • Used in cryogenics as a refrigerant.
  • Used in gas lasers and as a component in some plasma displays.

Interesting Facts

  • Neon lights actually emit red-orange light; other colors are due to other gases or coatings.
  • It’s chemically inert and forms no stable compounds under normal conditions.
  • It is the fifth most abundant element in the universe.

Did You Know?

The name “Neon” comes from the Greek word *neos*, meaning "new". Even though it's colorless as a gas, it glows brilliantly in electric discharge!
