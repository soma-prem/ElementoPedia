Nitrogen - Element Details

Symbol: N

Atomic Number: 7

Atomic Mass: 14.007 u

Electron Configuration: 1s2 2s2 2p3

Group: 15 (Pnictogens)

Period: 2

Block: p-block

Category: Non-metal

State at Room Temperature: Gas

Color: Colorless (gas)

Melting Point: -210 °C

Boiling Point: -196 °C

Discovery

Nitrogen was discovered in 1772 by Scottish physician Daniel Rutherford, who removed oxygen and carbon dioxide from air and showed that the remaining gas could not support life or combustion.

Abundance & Sources

Nitrogen makes up about 78% of the Earth's atmosphere by volume. It is obtained by the fractional distillation of liquid air.

Uses

  • Used in the production of ammonia for fertilizers via the Haber process.
  • As a protective gas in food packaging to prevent spoilage.
  • Liquid nitrogen is used as a cryogen for freezing and storing biological samples.
  • Used in electronics, explosives, and welding.

Interesting Facts

  • Nitrogen is essential for life — it's a major part of amino acids, proteins, and DNA.
  • Despite its abundance, nitrogen must be fixed (converted) into usable forms by bacteria or industrial processes.
  • It is extremely inert in its gaseous diatomic form (N₂) due to a strong triple bond.

Did You Know?

Lightning can break the strong N≡N bond in atmospheric nitrogen, allowing it to combine with oxygen and form nitrogen oxides — a natural form of nitrogen fixation.
