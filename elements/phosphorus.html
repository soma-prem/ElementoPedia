Phosphorus – Element Details

Symbol: P

Atomic Number: 15

Atomic Mass: 30.974 u

Electron Configuration: [Ne] 3s2 3p3

Group: 15 (Pnictogens)

Period: 3

Category: Non‑metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Melting Point (white P): ≈ 44.15 °C

Boiling Point: ≈ 280.5 °C

Discovery

Phosphorus was discovered in 1669 by Hennig Brandt—who distillated urine to isolate the glowing substance he called “cold fire.” The name means “light-bearer” in Greek. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Abundance & Sources

Do not occur free in nature. Extracted from phosphate rocks (e.g. apatite) via electric furnace methods. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Uses

  • Main ingredient in fertilizers (phosphates)
  • Red phosphorus used in safety matches, fireworks
  • Phosphor bronze in electrical components

Interesting Facts

  • Fourth most abundant element in living organisms (in DNA, ATP, membranes) ⬅ essential biochemical roles :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
  • Exists in several allotropes: white, red, black phosphorus
  • White phosphorus glows faintly in air and ignites spontaneously

Did You Know?

Phosphorus was the first element discovered chemically (not known since antiquity). Brand distilled ~50 buckets of urine to produce ~120 g of the element. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
