Platinum - Element Details

Symbol: Pt

Atomic Number: 78

Atomic Mass: 195.084 u

Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f¹⁴ 5d⁹ 6s¹

Group: 10 (Transition Metals)

Period: 6

Block: d-block

Category: Transition Metal, Platinum Group Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Silvery-white, malleable, and ductile metal

Density: ~21.45 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~1768 °C

Boiling Point: ~3825 °C

Discovery

Platinum was known and used by pre-Columbian peoples in South America for thousands of years. It was first described in Europe by Spanish naval officer **Antonio de Ulloa** in 1748, who found it in Colombia. He named it "platina," meaning "**little silver**," as it was considered an unwanted impurity in the silver he was mining. The metal's true value was not recognized until much later.

Abundance & Sources

Platinum is one of the rarest elements in the Earth's crust. It is a member of the platinum group metals and is often found in alluvial deposits. The largest reserves are found in the Bushveld Complex in **South Africa**, which accounts for over 70% of global production. Other significant sources are in Russia and Zimbabwe, and it is also obtained as a byproduct of nickel and copper mining.

Industrial Preparation

The extraction and refining of platinum is a complex and lengthy process. The ore is mined, crushed, and then concentrated through a flotation process. The resulting concentrate is melted in a furnace to remove some impurities, and then the platinum group metals are separated from each other through a series of chemical processes, including dissolution in strong acids like aqua regia and subsequent precipitation.

Uses

  • The primary use of platinum is in **catalytic converters** for vehicles to reduce harmful exhaust emissions, accounting for over 40% of its demand.
  • It is a major material for **jewelry** due to its natural bright white color, tarnish resistance, and durability.
  • Platinum is an excellent **catalyst** and is used in a wide range of industrial chemical processes, including the production of nitric acid, silicones, and high-octane gasoline.
  • Its biocompatibility makes it suitable for **medical implants** and surgical instruments, and platinum compounds are important **chemotherapy drugs** used to treat certain cancers.
  • It is also used in electronics for electrodes, electrical contacts, and hard drives.

Interesting Facts

  • Platinum is one of the densest elements, at 21.45 g/cm³, making it nearly twice as dense as gold.
  • It is a noble metal, meaning it is highly resistant to corrosion and oxidation, even at high temperatures.
  • The total amount of platinum ever mined could fit into a cube roughly 25 feet on each side.

Did You Know?

Because of its extreme resistance to corrosion and wear, an alloy of platinum and iridium was used to create the original international standard **meter bar** and **kilogram mass**, which were the world's official units of measurement for decades.
