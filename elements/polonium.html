Polonium - Element Details

Symbol: Po

Atomic Number: 84

Atomic Mass: [209] u

Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f¹⁴ 5d¹⁰ 6s² 6p⁴

Group: 16 (Poor Metals)

Period: 6

Block: p-block

Category: Poor Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Silvery metal (rarely seen due to radioactivity)

Density: ~9.196 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~254 °C

Boiling Point: ~962 °C

Discovery

Polonium was discovered in 1898 by the brilliant Polish-French scientist **Marie Curie** and her husband **Pierre Curie**. They isolated it from the uranium ore pitchblende, and it was the first element they discovered on their path to discovering radium. Marie Curie named it after her native country, **Poland**.

Abundance & Sources

Polonium is an extremely rare element in nature. It is found in minute, trace amounts in uranium ores as a product of the natural radioactive decay of uranium and radium. However, its primary source for human use is artificial production in nuclear reactors.

Industrial Preparation

The most common method for producing polonium is by bombarding a stable bismuth-209 target with neutrons in a nuclear reactor. This process creates the isotope bismuth-210, which then decays with a half-life of 5 days into the desired isotope, **polonium-210**.

Uses

  • Polonium is a powerful **alpha particle source** and is used in anti-static devices for industrial processes and for cleaning dust off photographic film.
  • The heat-generating property of polonium from its radioactive decay is being explored as a compact, reliable heat source for **space probes** and other remote equipment.
  • Polonium has been used in spark plugs for vehicles, though this use is no longer common.
  • The element is also a subject of research in nuclear physics and radiation therapy.

Interesting Facts

  • Polonium is a **highly radioactive and toxic element**. Its most common isotope, Po-210, is extremely dangerous even in minute quantities and is fatal if ingested.
  • It is the only element that can form a simple cubic crystal structure, a unique property in the periodic table.
  • Due to its intense alpha radiation, a small piece of polonium-210 glows in the dark with a bluish light from the ionization of the surrounding air.

Did You Know?

Polonium-210 has a unique property of generating a large amount of heat from its radioactive decay. A single gram of pure polonium-210 can reach a temperature of 500 °C and produces about 140 watts of thermal power, making it a very efficient and dangerous heat source.
