Potassium – Element Details

Symbol: K

Atomic Number: 19

Atomic Mass: 39.098 u

Electron Configuration: [Ar] 4s1

Group: 1 (Alkali Metals)

Period: 4

Category: Alkali Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Silvery‑white metal

Melting Point: 63.5 °C

Boiling Point: 759 °C

Density: ~0.89 g/cm³

Discovery

Potassium was first isolated by Sir Humphry Davy in 1807 via electrolysis of molten potassium hydroxide :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.

Abundance & Sources

Potassium is the seventh most abundant element in Earth's crust. It occurs in minerals such as sylvite, carnallite, and potash. Commercially used derivatives include potassium chloride and carbonate :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

Uses

  • Main component in fertilizers (potassium salts) :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.
  • Used in glass manufacture and soaps (potassium carbonate/hydroxide).
  • Potassium chloride used in medical injections and salt substitutes.
  • Used as coolant in nuclear reactors.

Interesting Facts

  • Highly reactive—reacts violently with water, producing hydrogen gas and heat :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.
  • Name derives from “potash”; symbol K from Latin “kalium” :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.

Did You Know?

The bright yellow color of streetlamp sodium flame tests include potassium’s signature flame spectrum (~766 nm).
