Scandium - Element Details

Symbol: Sc

Atomic Number: 21

Atomic Mass: 44.96 u

Electron Configuration: [Ar] 3d¹ 4s²

Electron Shells: 2, 8, 9, 2

Group: 3 (Transition metals)

Period: 4

Category: Transition Metal / Rare Earth

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Density: ~3.0 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~1541 °C

Boiling Point: ~2836 °C

Discovery & Naming

First isolated in 1879 by Lars Fredrik Nilson from minerals euxenite and gadolinite. Its existence had been predicted by Mendeleev in 1869 as "ekaboron" and named after Scandinavia. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Sources & Abundance

Scandium is scarce—it's the 50ᵗʰ most abundant in Earth's crust (~22 ppm) and mainly extracted as a by-product of uranium ore refining. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Uses

  • Added (<0.5 %) to aluminium alloys in aerospace & sports gear: bicycle frames, baseball bats, high-intensity lamps. :contentReference[oaicite:3]index=3
  • Used in metal halide lamps to simulate sunlight. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  • Scandium-triflate serves as a Lewis-acid catalyst in organic synthesis. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Interesting Facts

  • Exhibits a +3 oxidation state exclusively. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
  • Its properties closely resemble rare-earth metals despite being in group 3. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Did You Know?

Lightweight like aluminium but far stronger—scandium remains too costly for widespread use, though Sc-Al alloys are prized for high-performance applications. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
