Silicon – Element Details

Symbol: Si

Atomic Number: 14

Atomic Mass: 28.0855 u

Electron Configuration: [Ne] 3s2 3p2

Group: 14 (Carbon family)

Period: 3

Block: p‑block

Category: Metalloid

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Density: ~2.33 g/cm³

Melting Point: 1414 °C

Boiling Point: 3265 °C

Discovery

First isolated in 1824 by Jöns Jacob Berzelius via reduction of silica. Earlier impure forms had been noted in 1811. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Abundance & Sources

Silicon is the second most abundant element in Earth's crust (~27.7%), used widely in electronics and glass manufacture. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Uses

  • Primary material in semiconductors, solar cells, integrated circuits
  • Used in glass, ceramics, silicones
  • Added in ferrosilicon steel alloys

Interesting Facts

  • Crystalline silicon is a hard, brittle semiconductor with metallic lustre. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
  • Most ferrosilicon and aluminium‑silicon alloys rely on elemental silicon. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Did You Know?

Silicon is essential to modern electronics—not just the 20th century semiconductor age, but also in glass, concrete, and the very structure of life.
