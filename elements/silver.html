Silver - Element Details

Symbol: Ag

Atomic Number: 47

Atomic Mass: 107.868 u

Electron Configuration: [Kr] 4d¹⁰ 5s¹

Group: 11 (Transition Metals)

Period: 5

Block: d-block

Category: Transition Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Silvery-white, lustrous metal

Density: ~10.49 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~961.78 °C

Boiling Point: ~2162 °C

Discovery

Silver has been known since ancient times and has been used by humans for thousands of years. It is one of the "seven metals of antiquity" mentioned in historical texts, and its discovery is not attributed to a single person. It was first mined in Anatolia (modern-day Turkey) as early as 3000 BCE.

Abundance & Sources

Silver is a relatively rare element in the Earth's crust. It is found in both its native, free form and in ores, most commonly as a byproduct of copper, lead, and zinc mining. The largest reserves are found in Mexico, Peru, China, and Australia.

Industrial Preparation

Silver is typically extracted from its ores using methods like the Parkes process (for lead ores) or the cyanide process, which involves dissolving the silver in a cyanide solution. It is then precipitated out of the solution using zinc or by electrolysis to obtain pure silver.

Uses

  • Silver is widely used in jewelry and silverware due to its beauty and durability.
  • It is an essential component in photographic film and plates, although its use has decreased with the rise of digital photography.
  • Due to its excellent electrical conductivity, it is used in electrical contacts, circuits, and conductors.
  • Silver is used in mirrors and optical coatings because of its high reflectivity.
  • It has antimicrobial properties and is used in water purification filters, medical applications, and in some clothing.

Interesting Facts

  • Silver is the best conductor of electricity and heat among all metals.
  • In ancient times, silver was often considered more valuable than gold in some cultures.
  • Silver tarnishes when exposed to sulfur compounds in the air, forming a black layer of silver sulfide.

Did You Know?

Silver has powerful antimicrobial properties. Colloidal silver was historically used as an antiseptic and is still used today in some medical and water purification applications.
