Sodium - Element Details

Symbol: Na

Atomic Number: 11

Atomic Mass: 22.9898 u

Electron Configuration: [Ne] 3s1

Group: 1 (Alkali Metals)

Period: 3

Block: s-block

Category: Alkali Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Silvery-white

Melting Point: 97.794 °C

Boiling Point: 882.94 °C

Discovery

Sodium was first isolated in 1807 by Sir Humphry Davy via electrolysis of molten sodium hydroxide :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.

Abundance & Sources

It is the sixth most abundant element in Earth's crust (~2.8%) and found widely in nature as sodium chloride. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Uses

  • Used as a coolant in some nuclear reactors.
  • Metallic sodium is used in high-pressure sodium-vapor lamps for street lighting.
  • Sodium salts like NaCl (table salt) and sodium carbonate are widely used in food, cleaning, and industry.

Interesting Facts

  • Metallic sodium is soft enough to cut with a knife at room temperature.
  • Its atomic emission produces the bright yellow flame used in flame tests (~589 nm).
  • Excess sodium intake can raise blood pressure; yet it's essential in physiological function.

Did You Know?

The name sodium comes from the Latin *natrium*; the element plays a key role in nerve conduction and fluid balance.
