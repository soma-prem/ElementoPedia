Titanium - Element Details

Symbol: Ti

Atomic Number: 22

Atomic Mass: 47.867 u

Electron Configuration: [Ar] 3d² 4s²

Group: 4 (Transition Metals)

Period: 4

Category: Transition Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Density: ~4.5 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~1660 °C

Boiling Point: ~3287 °C

Discovery

Discovered by William Gregor in 1791 and named by Martin Klaproth in 1795. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Abundance & Sources

Comprises ~0.44% of Earth's crust. Extracted from minerals such as ilmenite and rutile. Production via Kroll process yields pure titanium. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Uses

  • Aerospace & military: airframes, engines, spacecraft due to its strength‑to‑weight ratio. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
  • Medical implants & instruments thanks to biocompatibility and corrosion resistance. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
  • Marine equipment, high-performance sports gear, jewelry (titanium alloys). :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

Interesting Facts

  • About 45% lighter than steel but equally strong, and more resistant to corrosion. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}
  • Forms a protective oxide layer (TiO₂), making it ideal against seawater corrosion. :contentReference[oaicite:15]{index=15}
  • Universally biocompatible; widely used for hip, dental and spinal implants. :contentReference[oaicite:16]{index=16}

Did You Know?

The Ti‑6Al‑4V alloy is one of the most popular materials in aerospace and biomedical applications due to its combination of strength, ductility, and biocompatibility. :contentReference[oaicite:17]{index=17}
