Zinc - Element Details

Symbol: Zn

Atomic Number: 30

Atomic Mass: 65.38 u

Electron Configuration: [Ar] 3d¹⁰ 4s²

Group: 12 (Post-transition Metals)

Period: 4

Block: d-block

Category: Post-transition Metal

State at Room Temperature: Solid

Color: Bluish-white, lustrous metal

Density: ~7.134 g/cm³

Melting Point: ~419.5 °C

Boiling Point: ~907 °C

Discovery

While zinc compounds were used in ancient times, the metal itself was not recognized as a distinct element until later. Pure metallic zinc was produced in India around the 12th century. In Europe, it was discovered by German chemist Andreas Marggraf in 1746.

Abundance & Sources

Zinc is the 24th most abundant element in the Earth's crust. It is typically found in sulfide ores, with sphalerite (zinc blende, ZnS) being the most common. Major zinc mining countries include China, Australia, and Peru.

Industrial Preparation

Zinc is extracted from its ores primarily by two main methods: pyrometallurgical (roasting to zinc oxide, then reduction with carbon) and hydrometallurgical (leaching with sulfuric acid, then electrolysis).

Uses

  • Used extensively for galvanizing steel and iron to prevent rust and corrosion.
  • Forms alloys, most notably brass (with copper), used in musical instruments, hardware, and decorative objects.
  • Zinc oxide (ZnO) is used in rubber manufacturing, as a pigment (zinc white), in sunscreens, and as a mild antiseptic.
  • Zinc is an essential micronutrient for humans, animals, and plants, crucial for immune function, wound healing, and DNA synthesis.
  • Used in die-casting for automotive parts, household fixtures, and toys.

Interesting Facts

  • Zinc can be very brittle at room temperature but becomes malleable and ductile when heated to between 100°C and 150°C.
  • The human body contains about 2-3 grams of zinc, vital for the function of over 300 enzymes.
  • When zinc reacts with acids, it produces hydrogen gas.

Did You Know?

Galvanization, the process of applying a protective zinc coating to steel or iron, is one of the most effective and widely used methods to prevent corrosion, significantly extending the lifespan of metal products.
